EU regulators open inquiry into AI-driven price discrimination

The European Commission has opened an inquiry into how AI-driven dynamic pricing affects consumer rights, focusing on real-time personalization.

By Marta Reyes May 13, 2026 · 4 min read

The European Commission today announced a formal inquiry into the use of AI-driven dynamic pricing by major online retailers, citing concerns about transparency, consumer protection, and the potential for algorithmic discrimination.

The investigation will examine whether real-time personalization — adjusting prices based on a shopper's browsing history, device, location, or inferred willingness to pay — constitutes unfair commercial practice under the 2024 AI Act.

"Consumers have a right to know whether the price they see is the same one their neighbor sees," said Commissioner Klara Voss in a press briefing in Brussels.

Industry response was mixed. The Online Retail Federation said most members already publish price-change logs voluntarily; consumer groups argued voluntary disclosure has been "incomplete and opaque."

The Commission expects to publish preliminary findings by Q4 2026.
